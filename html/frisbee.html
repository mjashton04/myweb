FUN FACTS

01
No Referees
Ultimate is self-officiated. Players call their own fouls and resolve disputes themselves — it's built on a concept called "Spirit of the Game."
02
Invented at a High School
Joel Silver invented Ultimate Frisbee in 1968 at Columbia High School in New Jersey. The first college game was played between Princeton and Rutgers in 1972.
03
The Disc Can't Move Backwards
You can't run with the disc. When you catch it, you stop and have 10 seconds to throw. The only way to advance is passing forward — just like football.
04
Olympic Recognition
The World Flying Disc Federation (WFDF) has been recognized by the International Olympic Committee since 2015, paving the way for future Olympic inclusion.
05
Mixed Gender by Design
Many Ultimate leagues are mixed-gender by default. The highest level of competition has a division specifically for mixed-gender teams.
06
Wrist Snap is Everything
The power behind a disc throw comes almost entirely from wrist snap, not arm strength. A good flick throw can travel 50+ meters with just your wrist.
07
Named After a Pie Tin
The Frisbee is named after the Frisbie Pie Company. College students in the 1940s tossed empty pie tins around and yelled "Frisbie!" as a warning.
08
Stall Count = 10
The defender guarding the thrower counts aloud to 10. If the disc isn't thrown by "Stall 10," possession switches — creating constant pressure and fast play.
09
Spirit of the Game
SOTG isn't just a rule — it's a philosophy. Even at world championships, players are expected to celebrate great plays by the other team. It's unique in competitive sports.
10
Pro Leagues Exist
The American Ultimate Disc League (AUDL) is a professional league with 20+ teams across the US and Canada. Games average 400+ passes per match.
11
World Championships
WFDF hosts World Ultimate Championships every four years. The USA has historically dominated, but Colombia's women's team is one of the top programs in the world.
12
Dogs Love It Too
Disc dog (Frisbee with dogs) is its own competitive sport with world championships. The record for a dog catching a disc is 122 meters — farther than most humans can throw.

how it works

CORE RULES

  • 1
    Score in the end zone Catch the disc inside the opposing team's end zone to score a point. First to the target score wins.
  • 2
    No running with the disc Once you catch it, you must stop and pivot. You have 10 seconds to throw — that's it.
  • 3
    Incomplete passes = turnover If the disc hits the ground, flies out of bounds, or gets intercepted, possession immediately goes to the other team.
  • 4
    Non-contact sport You cannot make contact with the thrower or push through a catcher. Fouls are called by players, not refs.
  • 5
    Spirit of the Game All players are expected to uphold sportsmanship, resolve disputes fairly, and respect opponents regardless of the score.
